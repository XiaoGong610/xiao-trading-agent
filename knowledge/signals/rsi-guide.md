# RSI (Relative Strength Index) Guide

## Basics
RSI measures momentum on a 0-100 scale over 14 periods. It tells you where you are in the buying/selling cycle — not whether a stock is "good" or "bad."

## Thresholds & Actions

| RSI | Signal | Action |
|-----|--------|--------|
| < 30 | **Oversold** | Best entries — selling exhausted, likely to bounce. Buy the fear. |
| 30-50 | Weak/Neutral | Good accumulation zone for DCA. Momentum is below average. |
| 50-70 | Neutral/Strong | Healthy trend. Normal DCA, no urgency to add. |
| > 70 | **Overbought** | Reduce buying, sell premium (CSP), or wait. Don't chase. |

## Context Matters

RSI alone doesn't tell you enough. Combine with:

- **Fwd P/E** — oversold + cheap = high-conviction entry. Oversold + expensive = could be oversold for a reason.
- **Trend** — RSI 30 in an uptrend (pullback) is better than RSI 30 in a downtrend (falling knife).
- **Earnings** — RSI can be misleading around earnings. A post-earnings gap down might show RSI 25 but the thesis could be broken.
- **Sector** — high-beta sectors (semis, crypto) routinely hit RSI extremes. Low-beta sectors (utilities, staples) rarely go below 35 or above 65.

## Common Mistakes

- **Buying solely because RSI < 30** — check WHY it's oversold. Thesis-breaking news ≠ buying opportunity.
- **Selling solely because RSI > 70** — strong trends can stay overbought for weeks. RSI 75 in a secular uptrend can go to 85.
- **Ignoring timeframe** — daily RSI and weekly RSI can tell different stories. We use daily (14-period) as default.
