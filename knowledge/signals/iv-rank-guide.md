# IV Rank & IV Percentile Guide

## What They Measure
- **IV Rank** — where current IV sits relative to its 52-week range (0-100). IV Rank 80 = current IV is near the top of its annual range.
- **IV Percentile** — what % of days in the past year had lower IV than today. IV %ile 90 = IV was lower than today on 90% of days.

Both tell you: **is premium expensive or cheap right now?**

## Thresholds & Strategy Selection

| IV Rank | Signal | Strategy Implication |
|---------|--------|---------------------|
| < 25 | **Low** — premium is cheap | Buy options (LEAPs). Don't sell premium — not enough reward. |
| 25-50 | Below average | Neutral. DCA/Buy & Hold preferred. CSPs viable but not exciting. |
| 50-75 | Above average | Good for selling premium. CSPs and CCs offer decent returns. |
| > 75 | **High** — premium is rich | Best for selling (CSP, CC). Don't buy options — overpaying for vega. |

## Decision Matrix

| IV Rank | RSI < 30 (oversold) | RSI 30-70 (neutral) | RSI > 70 (overbought) |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------------|
| < 25 | LEAPs — cheap options + oversold = buy the dip with leverage | Buy & Hold or DCA | Wait — overbought + cheap IV = no edge |
| 25-50 | DCA aggressively | DCA normally | Reduce/pause DCA |
| 50-75 | CSP at support | CSP or DCA | CSP at resistance (get paid to wait) |
| > 75 | CSP aggressively — best combo | CSP — rich premium | CSP far OTM — collect premium while it's expensive |

## Key Principles

1. **Sell when IV is high, buy when IV is low.** This is the core edge of options strategies.
2. **IV tends to mean-revert.** High IV Rank usually comes down within 30-60 days — this is why 30-45 DTE CSPs work.
3. **IV spikes around earnings.** This is artificial — "IV crush" after earnings destroys premium. Don't buy LEAPs right before earnings. Do sell premium before earnings (if you're comfortable with the binary risk).
4. **IV Rank vs. absolute IV.** A stock with 30% IV and IV Rank 90 has expensive premium *for that stock*. A stock with 80% IV and IV Rank 20 has cheap premium *for that stock*. Always use rank/percentile, not absolute IV.
