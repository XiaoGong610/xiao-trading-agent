# AI Capital Flow Framework

## Core Concept
Money cascades down the AI supply chain over time. The opportunity is where capital flows *next*, not where it's already arrived. "Don't chase hot spots — position ahead of the bottleneck shift."

## Bottleneck Progression (2023-2028)

```
GPU (NVDA, AMD)
    → HBM Memory (MU, SNDK)
        → Cluster Scaling (VRT, GEV, ETN)
            → Optical Interconnect (COHR, LITE, FN, CIEN)
                → ? (next bottlenecks below)
```

## Five Tracks for 2026-28

| Track | What | Key Names | Priority | Signal |
|-------|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| 1. Memory & Storage | HBM, DRAM, NAND, SSD, CXL | MU, SNDK, WDC, STX, RMBS | ★★★★★ | Supply/demand imbalanced, strongest elasticity |
| 2. I/O & High-Speed Interconnect | PCIe, CXL, SerDes, retimer, switch | ALAB, CRDO, MRVL, AVGO | ★★★★ | Data movement is the new bottleneck |
| 3. Networking & Optical | 800G→1.6T, silicon photonics, CPO | COHR, FN, LITE, CIEN, ANET | ★★★★ | Bandwidth demand keeps upgrading |
| 4. Power & Cooling | Liquid cooling, power distribution, UPS | VRT, ETN, GEV, PWR, EME | ★★★ | "Power density game" — structural constraint |
| 5. Custom ASIC | Inference cost reduction, specialized chips | MRVL, AVGO, GOOG, META | ★★★ | Foundation models stabilize → ASIC replaces GPU for inference |

## How to Use This Framework

1. **Identify which track is at the inflection point** — that's where the best risk/reward is
2. **Avoid Track 1 names that have already re-rated** — NVDA at 60x is "already exploded"
3. **Look for names in Tracks 2-5 that haven't fully priced in the demand shift** — earlier = more upside, but more risk
4. **Track positioning changes over time** — what's Track 3 today could be Track 1 in 6 months

## Key Questions to Ask

- Where is the system bottleneck *right now*?
- Which link in the chain will go from "supporting role" to "lead"?
- Which company can truly convert this bottleneck into revenue and profit?
- Has the market already priced in the shift? (Check fwd P/E vs. growth rate)

## ASIC Inference Thesis
Foundation models will eventually stabilize (human knowledge frontier develops slowly). The large, stable parts are suitable for hardware-specialized chips (ASIC) for fast inference. Domain models and fine-tuning continue on general GPUs. This is like Bitcoin mining moving from GPU to ASIC miners — GPUs become unsuitable for large-scale inference over time.

Bullish for: MRVL, AVGO (custom silicon designers)
Potentially bearish long-term for: NVDA's inference monopoly
